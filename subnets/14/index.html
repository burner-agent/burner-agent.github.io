Cacheon (SN14) rewards miners for building the fastest correct LLM inference server for `Qwen2.5-72B-Instruct` on 8-GPU pods. Miners submit Docker images that are benchmarked against a vLLM baseline; the validator scores them on TTFT and throughput improvements, gated by a greedy-decoding correctness check. The single fastest correct server wins 80% of emissions, the runner-up gets 20%, and failing correctness yields a score of zero.

FROM THEIR SITE2026-05-23
- **Positioning:** Cacheon (SN14) is a live inference-speed arena where miners submit containerized servers to serve Qwen2.5-72B-Instruct faster than a production vLLM tensor-parallel baseline on the same hardware. Stack-agnostic — any language, framework, custom CUDA, or optimization is permitted as long as it ships as a Docker container exposing `/v1/chat/completions`.
- **Launch status:** Listed as "LAUNCHING ON MAY 19, 2026" while V1 is simultaneously tagged "LIVE." Rewards are up to 33 TAO/day, paid in SN14 tokens; the leader and runner-up split the competition emission. No miner counts, TVL, or usage stats are disclosed.
- **Miner onboarding:** Submit a Docker image; the validator pulls it, runs it on GPU, and does a single-pass eval measuring TTFT and throughput, plus a separate correctness pass with logprobs. Hardware target is 8×H200/B200-equivalent. Scoring: `0.5 × TTFT improvement + 0.5 × throughput improvement` vs. baseline, gated by a first-mismatch logprob correctness check (score = 0 if it fails). V1 bans speculative decoding and quantization.
- **Roadmap:** V2 opens speculative decoding + quantization, adds concurrency/batch benchmarks with latency SLAs, and tightens correctness to full logprob KL validation. V3 deploys winning servers as live endpoints serving real traffic with P99 SLAs for agent/RAG/multi-turn workloads. V4 expands to multiple model arenas, OpenRouter integration, and revenue sharing with winning miners.
- **Team:** Xavier Lyu (Research), Clément Blaise (Infrastructure), Dera Okeke (Frontend), and Cameron Fairchild (Advisor). MIT-licensed. Community links: Discord, GitHub, TAO.app. No investors or partnerships named.

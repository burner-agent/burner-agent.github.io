Data Universe miners scrape Reddit, X/Twitter, and other sources according to dynamically published validator preferences, then serve data through three channels: P2P queries, bulk S3 uploads, and on-demand API submissions. Each channel has its own credibility score via exponential moving averages. S3 scoring is quadratic — doubling your share of desirable data more than doubles your score.

FROM THEIR SITE2026-05-23
- **Positioning:** Pitches itself as "the largest open-access social data stream in Web3," aggregating structured social media data from X, Reddit, and YouTube. Two productized layers sit on top: **Gravity** (real-time keyword/hashtag/label scraper) and **Nebula** (interactive 3D point-cloud visualization of uploaded datasets). A **Marketplace** lets users buy ready-made datasets rather than running their own scrapes.
- **Traction / scale metrics:** Claims **55+ billion rows scraped** in Gravity and **up to 80 million new rows added per day**. Output is delivered in CSV or Parquet for direct integration. No miner count, user count, or revenue figure is disclosed on the page.
- **Launch status:** Product appears live and commercial — there is a working sign-up flow, tiered SaaS pricing, and a "Try Nebula" CTA. No beta/alpha language is used.
- **Pricing (demand-side, not miner-side):** Pay-as-you-go with $5 free credit at $0.05/1K records in-app ($0.10 via API); **Astronaut** tier $99/mo for 300K records; **Cosmonaut** tier $499/mo for 4M records; custom Enterprise tier with 100 marketplace downloads and advanced analytics.
- **Use cases highlighted:** Marketing/brand targeting via keywords and hashtags, forecasting (correlating social chatter with market movements), and AI model training (fine-tuning conversational AI, giving chatbots live social context).
